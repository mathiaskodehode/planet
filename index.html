NEPTUNE

It's a planet, and it's blue

Far beyond the warmth of the Sun, Neptune spins in darkness, a stormy blue giant.

This website is dedicated to exploring Neptune, the distant and mysterious eighth planet from the Sun. Visitors can browse an engaging image gallery featuring stunning photographs and artistic renderings of Neptune’s deep blue atmosphere, powerful storms, and faint ring system.

A year there is extremely long One orbit around the Sun takes 165 Earth years. Since it was discovered in 1846, Neptune has completed just one full orbit.

It’s the farthest planet from the Sun Neptune is the 8th and most distant planet in our Solar System, orbiting about 4.5 billion km (2.8 billion miles) from the Sun.

It looks blue because of methane. Methane gas in Neptune’s atmosphere absorbs red light and reflects blue light, giving the planet its deep blue color.

Its largest moon is very unusual Neptune’s biggest moon, Triton, orbits backward compared to most moons. Scientists think it may have been a captured object from the Kuiper Belt.

It was discovered using math Unlike most planets discovered by observation, Neptune was predicted by calculations because of unusual movements in Uranus’s orbit.

Triton has cryovolcanoes Instead of lava, Triton erupts nitrogen gas and icy particles from cryovolcanoes.

Only one spacecraft has visited it So far, Voyager 2 is the only spacecraft that has flown past Neptune (in 1989).

Neptune’s gravity is similar to Earth’s Even though Neptune is much bigger, its surface gravity is only slightly stronger than Earth’s.

Neptune may have diamond rain Scientists think the pressure inside Neptune could turn carbon into diamonds, which may fall like rain deep inside the planet.
