NEPTUNE

The Most Distant Planet in the Solar System

Far beyond the warmth of the Sun, Neptune spins in darkness, a stormy blue giant.

This website is dedicated to exploring Neptune, the distant and mysterious eighth planet from the Sun. Visitors can browse an engaging image gallery featuring stunning photographs and artistic renderings of Neptune’s deep blue atmosphere, powerful storms, and faint ring system.

Only one spacecraft has visited it So far, Voyager 2 is the only spacecraft that has flown past Neptune (in 1989).

It was discovered using math unlike most planets discovered by observation, Neptune was predicted by calculations because of unusual movements in Uranus’s orbit.

Neptune has 14 known moons. The largest is Triton, which orbits the planet backwards compared to most moons in the solar system

Neptune may have diamond rain Scientists think the pressure inside Neptune could turn carbon into diamonds, which may fall like rain deep inside the planet.

Along with Uranus, Neptune belongs to a class called ice giants. Its atmosphere contains hydrogen, helium, and methane, and the methane gives the planet its deep blue color.

Winds on Neptune can reach over 2,000 km/h (about 1,200 mph)—faster than the speed of sound on Earth.

Neptune orbits the Sun at an average distance of about 4.5 billion km (2.8 billion miles). Because of this distance, one year on Neptune lasts about 165 Earth years.
